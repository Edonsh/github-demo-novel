Jay Catsby stood on the balcony of his grand mansion, gazing across the dark water toward the faint
 green light that flickered in the distance. To most people, his life seemed like a 
 dream—lavish parties, endless music, and rooms full of laughter. Yet beneath the 
 glittering surface, Catsby carried a quiet hope that had guided him for years.
  Everything he had built, every celebration he hosted, was tied to a single vision 
  of the future.

Long before the mansion and the crowds, Catsby was simply James Gatz, a young man with 
restless ambition and a belief that he could shape his own destiny. He reinvented 
himself piece by piece, transforming dreams into reality through determination and 
imagination. To Catsby, the past was never truly gone; it was something that could 
be reached for again, if one only tried hard enough.

Each night, as the last guests drifted away and the music faded, Catsby’s thoughts
 returned to Daisy. She represented more than a person—she embodied the promise of 
 everything he had worked for. 

Though the world around him sparkled with wealth and excitement, Catsby’s story
 was ultimately one of longing and belief. He lived in the space between memory 
 and hope, convinced that tomorrow could bring back what yesterday had taken away.
