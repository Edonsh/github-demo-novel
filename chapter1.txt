Chapter 1
Jay Catsby often found himself awake long after midnight, wandering through the quiet
 halls of his mansion once the parties had ended. The chandeliers still glowed faintly,
  and empty glasses rested on marble tables, reminders of the laughter and music that 
  had filled the rooms only hours before. In those silent moments, Catsby felt the 
  distance between the life he displayed to the world and the hope he carried within 
  himself.


He believed deeply in the power of reinvention. The boy he once was—poor, unknown, and
 full of impossible dreams—had refused to accept the limits placed upon him. Through 
 courage, ambition, and a relentless belief in a better future, he transformed himself 
 into the man everyone now admired. Yet even with his wealth and status, Catsby
  understood that some dreams required more than success to become real.

Daisy remained at the center of every plan he made. Every grand party, every carefully 
arranged meeting, and every hopeful glance across the bay carried the same silent wish. 
Catsby imagined a moment when the past could be restored perfectly, as if time itself
 would bend to his determination. To him, the idea of losing that dream was unthinkable.

Still, there were nights when uncertainty crept into his thoughts. The bright lights 
of his mansion could not completely silence the questions in his mind. He wondered 
whether 
the dream he chased was truly within reach or whether it existed only in the glow of 
memory and imagination.

Yet Catsby refused to abandon hope. The green light continued to shine across the
 water, small but steady in the darkness. To anyone else it might have seemed 
 insignificant, but to him it represented possibility. As long as that light 
 remained, Catsby believed the future still held the promise of everything he 
 had ever desired.
